BLUE TEAM

The Blue Team or Squadra Azzurra was an Italian contract bridge team. From 1957 through 1975, they won sixteen world titles.

There were three regular pairs on the team from 1964 to 1969 and in 1972: Walter Avarelli–Giorgio Belladonna, Pietro Forquet–Benito Garozzo, and Massimo D'Alelio–Camillo Pabis Ticci

In early years, Eugenio Chiaradia and Guglielmo Siniscalco played, and in late years, Dano De Falco, Arturo Franco, and Vito Pittalà played.

Spiritual father and long-time coach Carl'Alberto Perroux served as the non-playing captain through 1966.

TEAM

Walter Avarelli

Avarelli was born in 1912 and died in 1987. With the Blue Team, from 1956 to 1972, he won nine Bermuda Bowls and three World Team Olympiads. His longtime partner was Giorgio Belladonna. They used the Roman Club bidding system. Together, they improved and wrote a book on said system.

Giorgio Belladonna

Belladonna was born in 1923 and died in 1995. He is considered one of the greatest bridge players of all time. With Walter Avarelli and Benito Garozzo as his partners, he won sixteen world championships. Belladonna is the creator of the Roman Club bidding system. He even went on to create Super Precision, a complex, strong, club-based method.

Pietro Forquet

Forquet was born in 1925 and is still living today. He is 97 years old! He partnered with Eugenio Chiaradia, Guglielmo Siniscalco, and Benito Garozzo. Together, they won fifteen world championship titles. With his main partner Garozzo, he wrote a book on the Blue Club bidding system.

Benito Garozzo

Garozzo was born in 1927 and is still living today. He is 95 years old! He joined the Blue Team in 1961 as a last minute substitute. Today, Garozzo is considered to be the world's best bridge player. With his teammates Pietro Forquet and Giorgio Belladonna, he went on to win thirteen world championship titles.

HISTORY

Italy won its first European championship but lost to the United States in the second Bermuda Bowl in 1951. The members of this six-man team included Chiaradia, Forquet, and Siniscalco.

After this, Captain Perroux began making preparation for the Blue Team to win these events. Their breakthrough came in 1956 when they defeated Bermuda Bowl champion France. Then, they went on to defeat the American team in 1957. The team now consisted of the members Avarelli, Belladonna, Chiaradia, d'Alelio, Forquet, and Siniscalco.

From this point on, the Blue Team went to win twelve more world championships until 1969. This streak was only broken by a sixth-place finish in the 1960 World Team Olympiad.

The group retired in 1969. However, some members made a comeback! They won four more world titles in the years between 1972 and 1975.
